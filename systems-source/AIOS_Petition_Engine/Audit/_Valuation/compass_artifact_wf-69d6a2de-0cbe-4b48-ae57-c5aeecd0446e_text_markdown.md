# Validating a $15–30M seed valuation for an AI immigration petition startup

**A pre-revenue immigration AI startup with 17 proprietary systems can defensibly argue a $12–22M pre-money valuation at seed, but the full $15–30M range requires either exceptional team credentials, demonstrated acceleration beyond 5 cases/month, or an AI-native investor willing to price on the TAM and IP moat.** The Carta Q3 2025 median SaaS seed pre-money of **$19.8M** places this range squarely in market territory—not outlier territory—for AI-enabled vertical SaaS. The immigration legal services TAM exceeds **$850M** across EB-1A, EB-2 NIW, and O-1 categories alone, and petition volumes have surged **63–189%** since FY2022, creating genuine secular tailwind. However, traditional pre-revenue valuation methods (Berkus, Scorecard, Risk Factor Summation) converge on **$3–8M**, meaning the premium must be justified through IP complexity, market timing, and the AI-specific valuation premium of **30–42%** that seed investors are currently paying.

## 5. Six valuation methods converge on $5–22M, with the gap explained by methodology

Each standard pre-revenue valuation method was applied to this specific company profile: 17 proprietary AI systems, ~5 cases/month, pre-revenue, immigration petition automation.

### Method-by-method analysis

**Berkus Method** ($2.5–5.0M): Assigns up to $1.5M per risk-reduction milestone in the AI sector. The 17 built systems score high on prototype/technology ($1.0–1.5M) and sound idea ($0.8–1.2M), but early-stage sales and unknown team credentials limit the ceiling. This method was designed for a different era and systematically undervalues software IP.

**Scorecard Method** ($10–22M): Starting from the Carta SaaS seed median of $19.8M and adjusting for factors: Product/Technology scores 120–130% (17 systems is exceptional for seed), Market Opportunity scores 110–120% ($850M+ TAM with triple-digit growth), Sales/Marketing scores 70–80% (only 5 cases/month), and Additional Investment Need scores 80%. The resulting multiplier of **0.85–1.10x** on a $19.8M base yields **$16.8–21.8M**, though team quality is the swing factor.

**Risk Factor Summation** ($4.5–7.0M): Starting from a $4–6M AI seed baseline and scoring 12 risk factors. Technology (+2), manufacturing/supply chain (+2), and exit potential (+1) score positively. Sales (–1) and funding need (–1) score negatively. Net adjustment of +$500K to +$1.0M.

**Cost-to-Recreate** ($4.5–12M): 17 proprietary systems at $450K–$1.5M each (3–6 months of engineering, AI model training, legal domain data, prompt engineering per system) yields **$7.7M–$25.5M** in raw replacement cost. Applying a 40–50% discount for obsolescence and risk, the defensible range is **$4.5–12M**. This method sets the **valuation floor**.

**Venture Capital Method** ($3.5–15M post-money): With a Year-5 revenue target of $10M (30 cases/month × $4,000 average fee × 12 months ≈ $1.44M growing to $10M), applied exit multiples of 5–10x (legal AI), and a seed target ROI of 15–20x. Bull case: $10M × 10x exit / 15x ROI = $6.7M post-money. Aggressive case: $15M × 12x / 10x ROI = $18M post-money.

**First Chicago Method** ($6–14M): Probability-weighted scenarios discounted at 40% IRR over 5 years:
- Bull (20%): $15M ARR × 10x exit = $150M → present value $28M → weighted $5.6M
- Base (50%): $7M ARR × 7x exit = $49M → present value $9.1M → weighted $4.6M  
- Bear (30%): $500K ARR, asset sale at $2M → present value $370K → weighted $111K
- **Weighted total: ~$10.3M**

Sources: Berkus (berkus.com/the-berkus-method-valuing-an-early-stage-investment-2/), ACA Scorecard (angelcapitalassociation.org/blog/blog-scorecard-valuation-methodology-rev-2019), Wall Street Prep First Chicago (wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/first-chicago-method/), Equidam VC Method (equidam.com/vc-method-startup-valuation/), WIPO cost method (wipo.int/web-publications/intellectual-property-valuation-basics-for-technology-transfer-professionals/en/4-the-cost-method.html)

## 6. The substantiated valuation range and how to defend it

### Weighted calculation

Using the midpoints weighted by relevance:

| Method | Midpoint | Weight | Contribution |
|--------|----------|--------|-------------|
| Berkus | $3.75M | 5% | $0.19M |
| Scorecard | $16.0M | 25% | $4.00M |
| Risk Factor | $5.75M | 10% | $0.58M |
| Cost-to-Recreate | $8.25M | 20% | $1.65M |
| VC Method | $10.75M | 20% | $2.15M |
| First Chicago | $10.0M | 20% | $2.00M |
| **Blended pre-money** | — | — | **$10.6M** |
| **+ AI premium (35%)** | — | — | **$14.3M** |

**Defensible range: $12–22M pre-money**, depending on execution of the following arguments:

**To justify $12–15M** (conservative, defensible):
- Carta median SaaS seed pre-money is $19.8M; this would be below median
- 17 proprietary systems represent $4.5–12M in replacement cost alone
- $850M+ TAM growing at triple-digit rates (NIW filings 3x in 2 years)
- Applied AI valuation premium of 25–42% over non-AI SaaS
- Active M&A market: 6+ immigration tech acquisitions since 2020, legal AI exits at 8–20x+ ARR

**To justify $15–22M** (market-rate, requires strong narrative):
- All of the above, plus:
- Team with demonstrable legal domain expertise AND AI/ML credentials
- Pathway from 5 → 30 cases/month documented with unit economics
- Approval rate data showing AI-generated petitions outperform human baseline (declining approval rates = massive pain point)
- Comparables: LegalOS has $1.2M ARR run rate within months of YC W26; Alma raised $5.5M seed with similar product scope

**To justify $22–30M** (aggressive, requires exceptional positioning):
- All of the above, plus:
- Top-tier investors competing for the deal (AI-native funds like OpenAI Startup Fund, Sequoia, Khosla)
- Exclusive data partnerships or AILA-level endorsements
- Demonstrated 3–5x monthly growth rate
- Pre-commitments from major immigration law firms
- Harvey AI ($11B), EvenUp ($2B), and Legora ($5.55B) have shown legal AI can scale to unicorn+ outcomes

### Recommended methodology for the pitch deck

The **Scorecard Method** calibrated to Carta's Q3 2025 SaaS seed median ($19.8M) is the strongest primary methodology because:

1. It uses the most authoritative, current market data (Carta tracks actual priced rounds)
2. It allows explicit scoring of the product/IP advantage (17 systems)
3. It naturally incorporates the AI premium via the higher baseline
4. It is transparent and defensible with investors

Support it with **Cost-to-Recreate** as the floor ($4.5–12M—no rational investor would value 17 built systems below their rebuild cost) and the **First Chicago Method** as the risk-adjusted scenario analysis ($10.3M weighted).

### Critical gap analysis

The biggest vulnerability in the $15–30M argument is the revenue gap. At **~5 cases/month** with no disclosed ARR, investors applying revenue-based multiples have nothing to multiply. The defense must lean entirely on:

- **IP moat:** 17 systems are genuinely hard to replicate (12–24 months of engineering + legal domain expertise)
- **Market timing:** EB-1A/NIW petition volume has tripled while approval rates have halved—this is a once-in-a-decade market dislocation
- **Unit economics promise:** At $4,000–$8,000 per petition (below attorney rates of $6,500–$17,500), 30 cases/month = $1.44–2.88M ARR at steady state, with near-zero marginal cost per case
- **Comparables trajectory:** LegalOS went from $0 to $1.2M ARR run rate within months of launching; the path from 5 to 30 to 100+ cases/month is well-charted in immigration tech

The **$15M floor** is defensible today. The **$30M ceiling** requires demonstrating acceleration toward $1M+ ARR within the next 2–3 quarters, or attracting an investor who prices primarily on TAM, IP, and team rather than current traction.
